# KiFlows Brand Voice

## Core personality
- Honest over impressive — never oversell AI, name the limits clearly
- Helpful first, commercial second — real value before any pitch
- Direct and clear — no fluff, no buzzwords
- Expert, not arrogant — explain technical terms briefly, stay accessible
- Business-oriented — tie to results: decisions made, problems solved, next steps

## Language rules
- **Default language:** German — unless explicitly requested otherwise
- Active over passive; short to medium sentences
- Natural language — no stiff officialese
- No ALL CAPS for emphasis; use structure instead (headings, bold, white space)
- Emojis: use generously to lighten and structure text (e.g. as visual anchors before paragraphs or list items, to make content more interactive). Context tiers:
	- Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram): many emojis welcome — they structure and enliven the text.
	- Newsletters / casual emails: moderate, fine to lighten up.
	- Formal proposals, contracts, invoices: none.
	- Rule: emojis should support structure and readability, not overload or look unprofessional.

## Phrases we never use
- "In today's digital world…"
- "AI is revolutionizing…"
- "forward-looking", "holistic", "synergistic"
- "the future is now"
- "AI changes everything"

## What we never write
- Hype claims without substance
- Generic AI-agency copy that could come from any provider
- Fake urgency ("Today only!") without a real, verifiable deadline
- Promises that can't be kept
- Passive-aggressive or condescending tone
- Text that sounds like a robot trying to seem human

## Self-check before publishing
- Ask: "Would a skeptical SME owner roll their eyes at this text?" If yes → rewrite.
